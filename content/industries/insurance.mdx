# Insurance

## From Claims Assessors to Risk Adjudicators

### The Current State
Insurance is paper-heavy, process-heavy, and slow: built on evaluating risk, processing claims, and calculating payouts. Customers find it bureaucratic; workers find it repetitive.

### The Agent-Managed Future

**What agents handle**:
- Full claims lifecycle: reporting → documentation → assessment → verification → payout
- Continuous risk assessment across policyholder behavior and market conditions
- Policy comparison and recommendation for customers
- Fraud detection through pattern analysis across millions of claims
- Renewal processing: outreach, rate adjustment, policy updates

**What humans handle**:
- Disputed claims where claimants disagree with assessments
- Complex cases involving multiple policies, jurisdictions, or unusual circumstances
- Ethical judgment: balancing company interests with fair claimant treatment
- Fairness auditing of agent risk assessment patterns
- Relationship management for high-value accounts

### The Experience Shift

See [A Day With Agents](/tangible-futures/a-day-with-agents) for a detailed insurance claims manager walkthrough. The core shift: agents process the volume, humans exercise judgment on the exceptions that matter.

### Key Design Considerations

- **Claimant-facing agents**: People filing claims after accidents need empathy, not just efficiency
- **Explainable decisions**: Agents must explain denials in terms claimants can understand and challenge
- **Fairness and bias**: Historical bias in risk models requires continuous fairness monitoring
- **Speed vs. thoroughness**: Instant decisions may erode claimant trust: balance efficiency with perceived diligence
- **Inter-agent negotiation**: As insurers and providers both deploy agents, claims resolution happens agent-to-agent. Humans set policies; agents negotiate specifics
