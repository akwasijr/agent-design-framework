# Manufacturing

## From Plant Operators to Systems Conductors

### The Current State
Manufacturing is highly structured: production monitoring, supply chains, quality control, maintenance, logistics: but requires constant human attention to prevent and respond to disruptions.

### The Agent-Managed Future

**What agents handle**:
- Production optimization: adjusting parameters from real-time sensor data and demand signals
- Supply chain orchestration: procurement, logistics, and inventory across global networks with real-time disruption response
- Predictive maintenance: scheduling repairs before failures occur
- Quality assurance: real-time defect detection, root cause analysis, process adjustment
- Sustainability optimization: energy, waste, and carbon footprint tracking alongside production
- Digital twin management: simulating changes before physical implementation

**What humans handle**:
- Strategic decisions: product mix, capacity planning, new product introduction
- Disruption response requiring creative problem-solving
- Safety oversight of agent-managed processes
- Workforce management: teams, training, culture
- Innovation: process redesign and new technology adoption

### A Day in the Life

Morning briefing: efficiency at 94.3%, predictive maintenance scheduled with no production impact, supply chain rerouted around a port delay. One decision needed: demand forecast shows a 30% spike, begin capacity adjustment? You make the strategic call. Agents monitor every sensor and machine, optimise schedules, and flag only what needs human judgment.

### Key Design Considerations

- **Physical-digital bridge**: The interface between agent decisions and physical execution (robots, machinery, workers) is a critical challenge
- **Safety criticality**: Safety guardrails must be absolute, not configurable
- **Real-time response**: Production adjustments can't wait for human approval: autonomy models must account for this
- **Workforce transition**: Design must be accessible to workers without extensive digital tool experience
- **Legacy systems**: Agents must interface with decades-old equipment never designed for agent interaction
- **Resilience**: Graceful degradation to human operation when agent systems fail is essential
