# Healthcare & Life Sciences

## From Clinical Practitioners to Care Orchestrators

### The Current State
Healthcare professionals spend disproportionate time on **administration**: charting, scheduling, billing, prior authorizations. Burnout is partly a documentation problem. Patients experience fragmented care across disconnected systems.

> 🔵 Agent-handled &nbsp; 🔴 Human-handled

**What agents handle**:
- Patient journey coordination: scheduling, reminders, follow-ups, referrals, care transitions
- Real-time clinical documentation from patient encounters
- Diagnostic support: analyzing labs, imaging, and patient history
- Drug interaction checking across all medications and conditions
- Population health monitoring: trends, outbreaks, at-risk populations
- Clinical trial matching for eligible patients

**What humans handle**:
- Complex diagnosis requiring intuition and deep clinical knowledge
- Procedures and physical care: surgery, examinations, hands-on treatment
- Empathy, reassurance, and human presence
- Ethical decisions: end-of-life care, resource allocation, informed consent
- Care team agent oversight and coordination

### The Experience Shift

See [A Day With Agents](/tangible-futures/a-day-with-agents) for a detailed hospital department head walkthrough. The core shift: agents handle documentation and coordination, clinicians focus on patients.

### Key Design Considerations

- **Life-or-death stakes**: Agent errors can harm patients. Clinical approval flows must be rigorous without creating bottlenecks
- **Health data sovereignty**: Agents need comprehensive data, but patients must maintain control
- **Cultural sensitivity**: Agents must be culturally competent, not just multilingual
- **The empathy question**: How should agents handle the emotional dimensions of health?
- **Clinician trust**: Doctors must trust agent recommendations without becoming dependent on them
- **Equity**: Agent-managed care must not widen health disparities: design for equity from the start
