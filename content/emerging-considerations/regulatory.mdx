# Regulatory & Compliance

## New Frameworks for Agent-Governed Work

### The Emerging Question
Current regulations assume **human decision-makers**. Agent-managed decisions in lending, healthcare, or education don't fit existing frameworks.

### What Needs Regulating

**Accountability**: Who's responsible when an agent decides wrong: deployer, organization, or creator?

**Bias and fairness**: Agents can amplify systemic biases. Regulations must define fairness auditing and remediation.

**Transparency**: "The algorithm decided" isn't sufficient. Explainability requirements need definition for agent decisions.

**Data handling**: Balancing broad data access with privacy, especially for cross-border operations.

**Safety-critical domains**: Healthcare, manufacturing, and transportation need testing, certification, and monitoring standards for agents.

### The Regulatory Landscape by Industry

| Industry | Key Regulatory Needs | Current Gaps |
|---|---|---|
| Banking & Finance | Fair lending, AML, capital requirements | Agent-specific audits, liability for agent trading |
| Insurance | Fair pricing, claims handling, solvency | Fairness auditing for agent underwriting |
| Healthcare | Patient safety, HIPAA, clinical standards | Agent clinical decision liability |
| Government | Due process, equal protection, transparency | Algorithmic accountability, right to human review |
| Education | Student privacy (FERPA), standards | Agent assessment validity, dependency concerns |
| Manufacturing | Workplace safety (OSHA), product safety | Agent-controlled equipment certification |

### Design Implications

Regulation is a **design requirement**:

- **Audit trails by default**: Every decision logged with full context
- **Explainability interfaces**: Show how decisions were made, in human terms
- **Compliance dashboards**: Real-time regulatory status visibility
- **Right to human review**: Path to human review of any agent decision
- **Regulatory update propagation**: Agent behavior updates traceably when rules change

### Designing Ahead of Regulation

Technology outpaces regulation. Agents will operate in gray areas for years. Design can bridge this by **building governance before regulations require it**. For example, ship every agent decision with a structured audit record (timestamp, inputs, model version, confidence score, outcome) now, even if no law mandates it. When explainability regulations arrive, you enable compliance instead of retrofitting years of opaque logs.
