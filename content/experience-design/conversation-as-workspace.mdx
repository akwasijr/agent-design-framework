# Conversation as Workspace

## Beyond Chat

Not chatbots: an **ongoing working relationship** expressed through dialogue, replacing point-and-click interfaces.

## What This Looks Like

### Persistent Context
A **continuous relationship with memory**, not disconnected requests:
- "Handle it like last time": the agent knows what you mean
- "The same approach we used for the Johnson account": context retained
- "I changed my mind about the conservative strategy": agent adjusts across all relevant work

### Intent Expression, Not Commands
Express **intent**, not step-by-step instructions:
- Not: "Open CRM, find accounts over $1M, filter by region, export CSV, create presentation"
- But: "Prepare a board deck on top accounts by region. Focus on growth trends."

### Multi-Modal Dialogue
- **Voice**: "Brief me on today while I'm driving"
- **Visual**: Charts and documents you respond to with annotations or selections
- **Gestural**: Pointing at a machine: "what's the status of this?"
- **Ambient**: Agent speaks through a smart device while your hands are occupied

### Negotiation, Not Configuration
Change agent behavior through dialogue, not settings pages:
- "Too aggressive with email follow-ups. Ease off."
- "No meetings before 10 AM."
- "Be more concise in summaries."

Over time, these negotiations create a personalized working style.

## Design Challenges

1. **Context persistence**: Maintaining relevant memory without clutter
2. **Modality switching**: Flowing naturally across text, voice, visual, and ambient
3. **Ambiguity resolution**: Clarifying intent without being annoying
4. **Escalation**: Moving from casual dialogue to formal review when needed
5. **Shared reference**: Building common vocabulary and shorthand over time

## What This Isn't

Not better chatbots: a **new medium for work** where the workspace is the conversation itself.
